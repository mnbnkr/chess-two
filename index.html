Rules

  • Pieces use shields: most survive one hit before they can be removed.
  • Each turn has one standard move and one Life/Death move. Players may skip the remaining Life/Death slot.
  • Life heals shieldless pieces on adjacent light diagonal squares. Death can kill adjacent non-King dark-square targets unless they have an orthogonally adjacent ally.
  • Life and Death are controlled by board half, can be passed through, and annihilate each other orthogonally. Death-hit attacks still land before the attacker dies.
  • Shield breaks use a staging square. Killing blows resolve on the destroyed piece's square.
  • Chess Two Knights use ramp jumps for empty-square moves and L-shapes for attacks. Toad-Fool and Frame Chess allow orthodox L-moves, and all Knight L-moves apply bend-square Life and Death pass-through effects.
  • A piece giving check becomes Intimidated, suppressing its shield while the check continues. The checked side's standard move must end the check by moving the King, blocking, or removing the checker.
  • Chess Two pawns can advance up to three squares from home. Toad-Fool Frontal Fan pawns move in a compact fan but attack by orthodox one-square diagonals. Pawns can use variant en passant and promote on the far rank.
  • Frame Chess suppresses shields on edge squares, blocks non-King standard attacks from or through the frame, and can wrap movement horizontally between the a and k files.
  • Kings are checkmated, not captured.
  • Only Kings and Life pieces remaining is a draw.
