Rules

  • Pieces use shields: most survive one hit before they can be removed.
  • Each turn has one standard move and one Life/Death move. Human players may skip the remaining Life/Death slot.
  • Life can heal shieldless adjacent pieces. Death can kill adjacent dark-square targets unless they have an adjacent light-square ally.
  • Life and Death are controlled by board half, can be passed through, and annihilate each other orthogonally. Death-hit attacks still land before the attacker dies.
  • Attacks use a staging square. Killing blows choose a resting square.
  • A piece giving check becomes Intimidated, suppressing its shield while the check continues.
  • Pawns can advance up to three squares from home, use variant en passant, and promote on the far rank.
  • Destroying a King ends the game immediately.
  • Only Kings and Life pieces remaining is a draw.
